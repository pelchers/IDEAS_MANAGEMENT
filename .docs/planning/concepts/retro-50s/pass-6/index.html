Kitchen Notes

Temperature tip: Always chill dough 30 minutes before rolling. Room-temp dough spreads too thin and cookies lose their shape. This is especially crucial for sugar cookies.

Icing consistency: Royal icing should be thick enough to hold a line for 10 seconds before melting back. Add water one teaspoon at a time for flood consistency.

I want to make a pie crust but I'm out of butter. What can I substitute?

Great question! Here are your best butter substitutes for pie crust:

  • Shortening (1:1 ratio) - Gives the flakiest crust, but less flavor. Use chilled.
  • Lard (1:1 ratio) - Traditional choice, incredibly flaky. Best for savory pies.
  • Coconut oil (use 3/4 the amount) - Freeze solid first, then grate into flour. Slightly sweet.

For best results with any substitute, keep everything ice-cold and handle the dough as little as possible!

I have shortening. How do I make the crust extra flaky?

Here's the secret to ultra-flaky shortening pie crust:

  1. Freeze the shortening for 15 minutes before cutting it in
  2. Leave pea-sized chunks - don't over-mix. Those chunks melt in the oven and create steam pockets = flaky layers
  3. Use ice water with a splash of vinegar (1 tsp) - the acid inhibits gluten formation
  4. Fold the dough like a letter 3 times before rolling - creates laminated layers

Chill the formed crust 30 minutes before baking. This relaxes the gluten and re-firms the fat!
