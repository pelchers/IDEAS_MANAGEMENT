Script Doctor

The third act feels flat. The detective's motivation isn't clear after the betrayal scene. How do we fix it?

MC
Mara Chen
SD
Script Doctor

The issue is that Lila's personal stakes vanish after Scene 47. Consider adding a callback to her opening monologue — she mentions her father's badge. If The Architect steals that badge in the betrayal scene, her pursuit becomes personal again, not just professional.

I like that. But the pacing of the final confrontation still drags. It's 12 pages of dialogue with no physical tension.

MC
Mara Chen
SD
Script Doctor

Break the 12 pages into three intercut threads: Lila and The Architect talking in the warehouse, the bomb squad outside on a ticking clock, and flashback fragments of their first meeting. Each thread raises tension independently, and you can crosscut to create rhythm without adding action to the dialogue itself.
