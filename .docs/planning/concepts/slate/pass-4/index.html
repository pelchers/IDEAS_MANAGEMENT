Painted Desert Corridor

Project Summary

The Painted Desert Corridor connects three previously isolated trail systems through a 12.4-mile point-to-point route traversing the colorful badlands formations of the northern sector. This project involves trail construction, interpretive signage, and habitat corridor preservation for pronghorn migration.

Seed Bomb Trail Restoration

Solar-Powered Trail Wayfinding

Install low-profile solar-powered LED trail markers that activate at dusk for safer sunset hikes. Markers use warm amber light to minimize wildlife disruption. Each unit is self-contained with a 5-year battery life and weatherized sandstone-colored casing to blend with the landscape.

Infrastructure 31 upvotes

Fossil Discovery Trail

Commission a short spur trail through the exposed Morrison Formation where visitors can see actual dinosaur fossils in situ. Interpretive panels explain how the desert landscape once looked like a lush floodplain.

Coyote Creek Wildlife Underpass

Install wildlife underpasses at three road crossings where camera data shows heavy coyote, deer, and tortoise movement. Reduces road mortality and reconnects fragmented habitat.
