What is the optimal balance spring material for a 4Hz flying tourbillon with minimal thermal drift?
14:22
For a 4Hz flying tourbillon, I recommend Nivarox Spiraflex — a silicon-based hairspring with a thermocompensation coefficient near zero. Key advantages:

Thermal stability: Silicon's CTE is 2.6 ppm/K vs. 12 ppm/K for Nivarox alloy — reducing rate variation from ±5 sec/day to ±1 sec/day across 8-38C.

Weight reduction: Silicon density (2.33 g/cm3) vs. NiSpan-C (8.1 g/cm3) reduces cage momentum by ~15%, improving positional accuracy.

Anti-magnetic: Inherently non-ferromagnetic, eliminating the need for a soft iron inner case.
14:22
JD
What about the brittleness concern? We had a silicon pallet fork fracture during shock testing last quarter.
14:25
Valid concern. Two mitigation paths:

1. DRIE-optimized geometry: Use Deep Reactive Ion Etching to create stress-relief fillets at attachment points. Patek Philippe's approach with Spiromax reduced fracture rates by 80%.

2. Hybrid approach: Silicon hairspring paired with traditional Glucydur balance wheel and metal pallet fork. You get the thermal benefits of silicon where it matters most (the spring) without exposing brittle material to the highest shock loads (the escapement).

Given your Ref. 7042's 253-component complexity, I'd recommend Path 2 for this commission and Path 1 for future iterations once we validate the geometry.
