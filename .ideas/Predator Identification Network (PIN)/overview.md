# Overview — Predator Identification Network (PIN)

## Problem
Independent predator-catching work is currently fragmented across personal social-media accounts, individual blogs, podcast episodes, and ad-hoc Discord groups. There is no shared, evidence-backed registry of confirmed predators that:

- separates **alleged** from **confirmed** from **convicted** with auditable evidence,
- protects the platform and innocent parties from doxing of unrelated individuals,
- gives investigators a sustainable revenue model so they can keep doing the work,
- gives the public a single trustworthy place to verify whether a person is an active predator concern.

The result is that good investigative work is invisible, bad-faith actors weaponize unverified accusations on social media, and predators benefit from the disorganization.

## Solution
**PIN (Predator Identification Network)** is a platform where individuals and groups who care about predator-catching can:

1. Register under a clear role (poacher, expert, journalist, legal rep, politician, content creator, general user, etc.).
2. Submit evidence and documentation against an individual.
3. Have that submission cross-confirmed by AI against supporting public evidence (court records, news, independent investigations) before the individual is publicly listed.
4. Maintain that individual's profile with status (post-conviction, currently prosecuted, independently confirmed, etc.) and a timeline of evidence updates.
5. Collaborate with others in a **group workspace** or operate as an **independent poacher**.
6. Publish documents, videos, and content — some free, some premium — under YouTube-style ToS that locates publishing responsibility on the poster.
7. Earn revenue from premium subscriptions, either per-poacher or via a sitewide subscription pool that distributes equitably.

## Why now
- Public appetite for predator-catching content is high and growing across YouTube, TikTok, and podcasts; the demand exists but is scattered.
- AI cross-referencing of public records, court filings, and news sources is finally cheap and accurate enough to gate publishing without crippling the workflow.
- The legal framework for hosted-content platforms (Section 230 in the US, similar safe-harbor regimes elsewhere) is well-understood — provided the platform itself does not direct the speech, it can host user-published claims with the poster carrying the legal exposure.
- Existing single-creator brands cannot scale a registry, and a registry is what gives the work durable value.

## Differentiators vs. social-media coverage
- **Structured registry, not feed.** Predator entries are first-class records with status, evidence, sources, and history — not posts that scroll away.
- **AI gate before public listing.** No predator is published until the AI cross-confirms supporting evidence. Failed submissions never reach the public explore page.
- **Multi-role identity.** Experts, journalists, and legal reps carry weighted review authority; their corroboration moves status faster than general-user corroboration.
- **Group collaboration.** Investigative groups get a shared workspace and shared credit — the same case can show contributions from multiple poachers without arguing over attribution.
- **Sustainable poacher economics.** Sitewide subscription revenue is pooled and distributed by engagement, so newer poachers can earn during their growth period instead of needing to ship their own paywall infrastructure.
- **ToS posture and takedown flow.** A clear hosted-content framing with appeal and counter-notice paths means innocent parties have a real remedy if mis-listed, and the platform has a defensible legal posture.

## High-level scope (v1)
- Public **explore page** of confirmed/listed predators with status filtering, search, and per-predator profile pages.
- Public **explore page** of poachers (individual + group) with stats and contribution history.
- **Submission flow** with AI cross-confirmation gate (anti-doxing safeguard).
- **Group workspaces** with member roles, document vault, and shared case timeline.
- **Content page** with documents/videos/evidence packets, searchable and filterable, with free and premium tiers.
- **Subscription tiers**: per-poacher subscription, sitewide subscription with equitable distribution.
- **YouTube-style ToS** with poster-responsibility framing, DMCA flow, takedown/appeal flow.
- **Strike system** + appeal flow + (optional) external review board for contested status changes.

## Out of scope (v1)
- Direct civil-suit assistance.
- Vigilante coordination / real-world meet-ups.
- Anonymous tip lines for active investigations (deferred to phase 2 with formal law-enforcement liaison framework).
- Native mobile apps — web-first, mobile-friendly responsive only.
